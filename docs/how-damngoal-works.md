What DamnGoal Is and How It Works

**damngoal!** helps you keep the dream and bend the plan — stay connected to what matters when life shifts, without streaks, guilt, or the pressure to perform every day.

You name a direction, show up in small ways through goals, habits, and next steps, and adapt when energy or circumstances change. Rest counts. Skips count as data, not failure. AI is there when you want help clarifying or adjusting — never to push, score, or shame you.

The Three Layers

DamnGoal is built around three things you can create. Together they connect long-term intention to what you actually do today.


---

Goals

What you do: Name something you want to move toward. It can be vague — "more energy," "get back to drawing," "figure out my career." Optional: why it matters, and tags from a fixed list (Health, Energy, Career, Creative, Relationships, Learning, Personal).

What DamnGoal captures:
- The goal title — your direction, not a SMART objective
- Why layer (optional) — private; resurfaced on low-motivation days
- Tags — static chips only, no custom tags
- State — where the goal lives in your life:
  - Active — working on it now
  - Adjusted — still pursuing, but something changed
  - Reached — you got what you came for, or you're living it now
  - Released (Resting) — not for you right now; not failure

How you create one:
- Manual — title required; why and tags optional
- AI — "Not sure yet? Talk it through" — a short conversation (3 steps: how you feel → direction options → confirm). Nothing saves until you say so.

What happens over time:
- Day 7–10: a gentle health check — keep going, adjust with AI, mark as reached, or let it rest
- Anytime from goal detail: mark reached, release, add habits, add tasks

Why it matters: Most apps treat goals as projects with deadlines. DamnGoal treats them as relationships — you can adjust, pause, reach, or release without breaking a streak that never existed.


---

Habits

What you do: Add a small recurring action — optionally linked to a goal. Pick how you track it: just show up (check-off) or spend time (target minutes). Optional: time of day, routine trigger chips (After coffee, After waking, Before bed — no location tracking).

What DamnGoal captures:
- Habit name
- Goal link (optional)
- Time of day — Morning, Afternoon, Evening, Whole Day
- Tracking type — check-off or timed (+ minutes)
- Routine trigger (optional) — single-select chip, no free text
- Lighter version — a smaller alternative for hard days (see Lighter Versions below)
- State:
  - Active — appears on Today each day
  - Paused — temporary break; can resume
  - Graduated — feels automatic; off Today, on Path
  - Released — done with this habit

How you create one:
- Manual — name required; everything else visible on the form
- AI — "Help me break this into small steps" (from a goal) or "Need help figuring this out?" (from habit form) — 2–3 suggestions with lighter versions included; tap to add

When is a habit "formed"? DamnGoal never auto-declares this (no 21-day badge, no streak threshold). You choose Graduate when it feels part of your life — it leaves Today, appears on Path, and counts in Insights. For staged goals, finishing a stage is the formation moment before the next unlocks.

Why it matters: Habits are the bridge between a vague goal and a concrete day. They stay small on purpose.


---

Tasks

What you do: Add a one-off action — for today or a future date. Same tracking types as habits: check-off or timed.

What DamnGoal captures:
- Task name (required)
- When — today (default) or pick a date
- Time of day — only when scheduled for today (default Whole Day)
- Tracking type and target minutes if timed
- Goal link (optional)
- Lighter version — for hard days and skip flow

How you create one:
- Manual — name required; when and tracking on the form; goal link below the fold
- AI — "Suggest something for today" — 2–3 energy-aware chips; tap to add (includes lighter version)

Lifecycle: Today or Coming up → done / partial / skip → off active lists → history in Insights. No recurring state.

Why it matters: Not everything deserves to become a habit. Tasks handle the one-offs without polluting your long-term list.


---

Today

What it is: The primary screen — labeled Today in the app. Your daily home.

What you see:
- Optional energy check-in — Good, Low, or Rough
- Progress strip — how many items you've touched today (not a streak)
- Habits and tasks grouped by time of day — Morning, Afternoon, Evening, Whole Day
- Coming up — future tasks, not mixed into today's timeline
- Pattern nudge — gentle contextual note when relevant

What you do:
- Mark items done, partial (timed only), or skip
- Expand lighter version pills on each item — auto-expanded on Low/Rough days
- Add goal, habit, or task via the Add button
- Do Nothing Mode or Focus mode from the header

Why it matters: Today is not a dashboard of overdue work. It's one day, right-sized to how you're feeling.


---

Energy Check-In

What you do: Tap Good, Low, or Rough — once, anytime, changeable.

What DamnGoal does with it:
- Good — full Today list
- Low or Rough — hides pending items that have no lighter version; keeps items you already touched today

What it is not: A permanent label, a personality type, or a score. Context for today only.

Why it matters: The app adapts dose to capacity — not willpower.


---

Lighter Versions

What they are: A smaller, specific alternative to the same habit or task — not a separate item.

Examples:
- "10-minute walk" → lighter: "5-minute walk around the block"
- "Drink water after waking" → lighter: "One sip at the sink"
- "Send thank-you note" → lighter: "Send a quick voice note instead"

Where they come from:
- AI at create — habit breakdown and task suggest (best quality)
- Rule fallback on manual create — timed >5 min → ~⅓ minutes; check-off → "Micro version"
- AI at edit — "Suggest a lighter version" on habit/task edit
- User edit — optional field on habit/task edit

Where you see them:
- Today tile — tap to expand (open by default on Low/Rough)
- Skip check-in sheet — offered when you skip
- Goal detail — on each habit card
- AI suggestion chips — before you add

Rules:
- Goals do not have lighter versions — only habits and tasks
- Without a lighter version, an item disappears on Low/Rough days (intentional)
- Doing the lighter version on a check-off item counts as Done, not Partial

Why it matters: Hard days are real. Lighter versions let you stay connected without pretending you have a full-capacity day.


---

Daily Completion

What you do: Tap an item on Today → Done, Partial (timed only), or Skip.

Check-off items: Done or Skip. Partial is hidden — a micro-version counts as Done.

Timed items: Done, Partial, or Skip. Partial counts as showing up.

What happens after:
- Done or partial → quiet micro-celebration + haptic
- Skip → soft check-in: what made today hard, lighter option, timing shift, or a motivational story

What it is not: A streak. A grade. A reset. Skipping does not undo prior progress.

Why it matters: Connection over completion. Showing up a little on a rough day is real progress.


---

Do Nothing Mode

What you do: Declare today a reset day from the Today header.

What DamnGoal does:
- Acknowledges rest — no guilt copy
- Clears Today actions for the day
- No reminders until you come back
- Gentle re-entry notification later

Why it matters: Rest is built into the system — not a failure state you hide from the app.


---

Path

What it is: A visual journey from present self to future self.

What builds the path:
- Graduated habits — steps you've moved to the background
- Reached goals — milestones you closed intentionally
- Bookends: where you started, where you're heading

What it is not: A streak calendar, a level system, or a completion percentage.

Why it matters: Progress you can feel without gamification. The path grows when you graduate and reach — not when you perfect every day.


---

Insights

What it shows: Awareness, not grades.

- Weekly summaries — showed up days, partials, skips
- Growth views — monthly, by tag, yearly
- Pattern notes — e.g. quieter Mondays, why layer resurfacing
- Monthly recap — consistency, adjusted/released goals, emotional arc

What it never shows: Compliance %, red/green scores, streak counts, leaderboards.

Why it matters: You see patterns without being scored by them.


---

AI Companion

What it is: Ongoing chat — not a coach, not a therapist. A thoughtful friend who reflects and asks.

What it knows: your goals, habits, recent skips, energy, why layer, patterns.

What it does:
- Suggest lighter versions
- Help adjust goals at health checks
- Offer contextual support after skips or low-motivation stretches
- Propose habit/task ideas when asked

What it never does: Cheerlead ("You got this!!!"), diagnose, assign user types, or save anything without your confirmation.

Entry points: FAB on Today and Goals tabs; goal health check "Adjust with AI"; Insights prompt chip.

Creation AI (separate flows, same tone):
- Goal clarify — conversation → draft goal
- Habit breakdown — 2–3 step suggestions from goal context
- Task suggest — 2–3 chips for today, energy-aware

Why it matters: Help when you want it. Silence when you don't. Always confirm before save.


---

Goal Health Check & Lifecycle

When: Around day 7–10 after creating a goal, or anytime from goal detail.

Options:
- Keep going
- Adjust with AI suggestions
- Mark as reached — positive closure
- Let this goal rest — released, not failed

Reached vs released:
- Reached — "I did it" or "I'm living this now" → Goals tab, Reached section + Path
- Released — "Not for me right now" → Goals tab, Resting section

Why it matters: Goals drift in real life. DamnGoal makes that visible and okay.


---

Skip Recovery & Low-Motivation Support

When skip patterns appear or motivation drops, DamnGoal offers (never forces):
- Lighter versions of today's items
- Timing shifts — try a different time tomorrow
- Motivational stories — real people, similar struggles
- Why layer resurfaced — your original reason, privately
- Pattern nudges — e.g. "Mondays seem heavier — plan a lighter start?"

Why it matters: Disengagement gets curiosity, not more reminders.


---

Social (Optional)

Goal network: Public goals visible to others. Reactions only — no comments, no DMs, no competition.

Sharing cards: Weekly summary for chosen friends — quiet witnessing ("Your friend showed up 5/7 days"), not a leaderboard.

Why it matters: Support when chosen. Never forced accountability.

How It All Connects

DamnGoal is not a collection of features. It is one system.

Goals — name the direction (vague is fine)
Habits & tasks — make today concrete
Today + energy — right-size the day to how you feel
Lighter versions — stay connected on hard days
Completion — done, partial, or skip — all count differently, none punish
Path & Insights — see growth without grades
AI Companion — clarify, suggest, adjust — when you ask

Do Nothing Mode — rest without leaving the system
Reached & graduated — close chapters without shame
Released & paused — pause without failure

The more honestly you show up — including skips and rest days — the more damngoal! can adapt. A goal pursued without guilt outlasts a goal pursued with a streak.
